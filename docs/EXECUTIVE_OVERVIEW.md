## 1. COMPANY SNAPSHOT

TrailLensHQ is a Canadian-owned B2B SaaS platform based in Guelph, Ontario that enables trail organizations to manage trail systems, coordinate volunteers, automate social media, and engage communities through an integrated web and mobile platform. Founded in 2026, the company is pre-revenue with MVP launching Q2 2026 through pilot organizations The Hydrocut and GORBA.

**Unique Position:** TrailLensHQ is the only all-in-one platform combining trail management, social media automation, and volunteer coordination—purpose-built for trail organizations rather than adapted from generic tools. Starting with Ontario mountain biking clubs, the company will expand to multi-province Canadian markets and U.S. trail organizations by Year 2-3.

**Key Differentiators:** Canadian-owned with local market expertise | Free tier for small nonprofits | Mobile-first design with offline support | Exceptional 22:1 LTV:CAC ratio demonstrating product-market fit

## 2. PROBLEM & SOLUTION

## The Problem

Trail organizations face critical inefficiencies managing fragmented communication tools. Trail managers spend 10-15 hours monthly on "communication overhead" using 4-6 disconnected platforms (Facebook, email, Instagram, Twitter, website) to reach trail users. Manual social media posting consumes an additional $2,000/month in volunteer time value. Volunteer coordination is scattered across email, text messages, and Facebook groups, resulting in only 20% member engagement vs. 40-50% target.

## The Solution

TrailLensHQ provides an all-in-one platform automating multi-channel communication, social media posting, and volunteer coordination. One trail status update automatically publishes to Facebook, Instagram, email, SMS, and push notifications within 2 minutes, reaching 100% of subscribers vs. 10-20% Facebook organic reach. The platform includes mobile apps for field reporting, trail work party scheduling, volunteer hour tracking, and analytics dashboards.

**Value Proposition:** Only platform combining trail management + social media automation + volunteer coordination. Purpose-built for trail organizations with free tier for small clubs, $49/month Pro tier, and mobile-first design with offline support. First-mover advantage in Canadian market with pilot customers The Hydrocut (100K visitors/year) and GORBA securing product-market fit validation.

## 4. PRODUCT & TECHNOLOGY

## Product Overview

TrailLensHQ is a multi-tenant B2B SaaS platform providing trail organizations with integrated trail management, social media automation, and volunteer coordination. Core components include trail system management with real-time status updates, Trail Care Reports (P1-P5 priority issue tracking with photo support), one-click multi-platform social posting (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter), multi-channel notifications (email/SMS/push), native iPhone apps (User + Admin with offline support), and web dashboards with 8 role-specific interfaces.

## Technology Stack

**Infrastructure:** AWS serverless architecture (Lambda, DynamoDB, API Gateway, S3, CloudFront, Cognito, SNS/SES) deployed in ca-central-1 (Montreal) for Canadian data residency. Year 1 infrastructure cost $5,316 vs. $50K+ traditional servers, with auto-scaling and 99.99% uptime SLA.

**Backend:** FastAPI (Python 3.13) with AWS Lambda deployment, DynamoDB single-table design, JWT authentication, 80%+ test coverage.

**Frontend:** React 18 + Tailwind CSS 3.4.13, deployed via AWS Amplify with CI/CD.

**Mobile:** Native iOS (Swift/SwiftUI) with Core Data offline storage, TestFlight distribution for MVP, Android (Kotlin/Jetpack Compose) in Year 2.

**Security:** Three authentication methods (Passkey/WebAuthn, Magic Link, Email/Password), MFA enforcement for admin roles, CloudTrail audit logging, AWS WAF OWASP protection, API rate limiting (100 req/min), SOC 2 Type II target Year 2.

## Roadmap

**MVP v1.13 (Q2 2026):** Trail management, social automation, notifications, iPhone apps, 8-role web dashboards, security hardening. Pilot customers: The Hydrocut, GORBA.

**Post-MVP (Year 1-2):** French localization (Q3 2026), Android apps (Q4 2026), advanced analytics, volunteer hour tracking, Trailforks integration, event management, community features, multi-region deployment for US expansion.

## 8. RISK FACTORS & MITIGATION

**Market Risks:** Small Ontario TAM (50-75 orgs) limits Year 1 growth, mitigated by multi-province expansion Year 2 (100-125 orgs) and US expansion Year 2-3 (2,000-3,000 orgs) | Longer sales cycles (6-12 months vs. 3-6 projected) addressed through free tier trial adoption and pilot customer testimonials | Seasonal volatility (April-October trail season) managed via annual contracts and winter activities (fat biking, skiing).

**Competitive Risks:** Trailforks could add B2B features (low likelihood, high impact) - mitigated by first-mover advantage, IMBA Canada partnerships, focus on organization management vs. trail mapping | Generic CRMs could add trail features (low likelihood) - mitigated by purpose-built platform, domain expertise, integrated 5-6 product modules, $49/month undercuts enterprise pricing.

**Execution Risks:** MTB product-market fit may not translate to hiking orgs - test hiking pilots Year 1, modular design allows customization | Technical delays risk - 20% cost contingency, AI acceleration, 60-70% codebase complete | Founder key person risk - first dev hire Month 7, AI reduces bus factor, advisory board recruitment, key person insurance.

**Financial Risks:** Economic downturn impact - free tier protects smallest clubs, outdoor recreation recession-resilient (grew 2020-2023), government contracts stable | CAD/USD volatility - price in local currency, natural hedge (CAD costs vs USD revenue), Year 1-2 Canadian focus minimizes exposure.

**Regulatory Risks:** Data privacy (GDPR, CCPA, PIPEDA) - AWS ca-central-1 Canadian residency, CloudTrail audit logging, WAF protection, SOC 2 Type II Year 2 | Quebec French requirements - full localization Year 2 ($15K-$25K), hire French-speaking customer success Year 1 end, start with English provinces Year 1.
